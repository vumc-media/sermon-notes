When It All Falls Apart: The Shattered Life
Job 1–2; Job 13:15
PT 1 — WHEN EVERYTHING FALLS APART AT ONCE (Job 1)
Some losses don’t come one at a time — they pile up. And when life collapses quickly, the hardest part isn’t only the pain… it’s the feeling of being unprepared for how fast everything changed.
What part of your life feels like it “fell apart all at once” — or faster than you could process?
When things started unraveling, what did you *want* God to do immediately?
What would it look like to be honest with God about the shock, without losing your grip on Him?
PT 2 — WHAT DOES THE BIBLE SAY ABOUT THIS? (John 16:33, 2 Corinthians 4:8, Job 2:10)
Scripture doesn’t pretend life is easy — it tells the truth about trouble, and it tells the truth about God. The Bible gives language for pain *and* a foundation for hope.
Which verse or phrase from today’s scriptures do you most need to hold onto right now?
Where are you tempted to interpret your suffering as “God must not care” — and what truth corrects that?
What is the difference between “accepting reality” and “giving up”?
PT 3 — FAITH THAT LOOKS LIKE JESUS
Faith that looks like Jesus doesn’t deny the cross — it keeps trusting the Father through it. Jesus shows us a faith that is honest, surrendered, and anchored… even when the outcome is costly.
What would “faith that looks like Jesus” look like in your situation this week?
Is there a place where God is inviting you to keep trusting — even if you can’t see the outcome yet?
Job 13:15 says, “Though he slay me, yet will I trust in him.” What does that kind of trust mean for you personally?
FINAL REFLECTION
When life shatters, faith isn’t pretending you’re okay. Faith is choosing not to let the brokenness be the final word. God can be trusted in the dark — and He can rebuild what feels beyond repair.
What is one prayer you need to pray honestly today?
